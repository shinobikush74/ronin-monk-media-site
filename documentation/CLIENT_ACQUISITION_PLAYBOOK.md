## MISSION

The target client believes in the power of story, world, and identity to build something real. They are a creator, a business owner, an organization, or a community that needs help discovering, articulating, or building their creative vision.

## THE ACQUISITION PIPELINE

Every prospective client moves through eight stages. No stage is skipped.

```
New Lead → Qualified → Discovery Call → Consultation → Proposal Sent → Negotiation → Client Won → Delivering
```

### Stage Definitions

| Stage | Definition | Action Owner | Time Limit |
|-------|------------|--------------|------------|
| **New Lead** | Name and contact in system | CRM Agent logs | Same day |
| **Qualified** | Lead meets basic criteria (need, budget intent, authority) | Creator confirms | 48 hours |
| **Discovery Call** | 30-min conversation to understand the vision | Creator conducts | 72 hours from qualified |
| **Consultation** | Deep-dive session OR proposal preparation | Creator/Agent | 5 days from Discovery |
| **Proposal Sent** | Formal proposal delivered | Creator approves/sends | 24 hours from consult |
| **Negotiation** | Scope or price discussion | Creator leads | 7 days max |
| **Client Won** | Agreement confirmed, deposit received | Creator closes | — |
| **Delivering** | Active engagement in progress | Creator executes | Per contract |

---

## QUALIFICATION CRITERIA

A lead becomes **Qualified** when they meet at least three of the following:

1. Has expressed a specific creative or brand challenge
2. Has a project with a timeline
3. Has decision-making authority (not "let me check with my team" without follow-up)
4. Has indicated willingness to invest (even vague: "what does something like this cost?")
5. Operates in an aligned industry (creative, cultural, storytelling, brand, music, publishing)

**A lead who does not qualify within 14 days is archived, not deleted.**

## DISCOVERY CALL PROTOCOL

The Discovery Call is the most important 30 minutes in client acquisition. It is not a sales call. It is a listening session.

### Objectives
1. Understand what the prospect is trying to build
2. Understand what is stopping them
3. Determine if Ronin Monk Media can genuinely help
4. If yes: schedule next step. If no: refer generously.

### Five Discovery Questions
1. "What are you building, and what does it need to become?"
2. "What have you already tried?"
3. "What would success look like in 90 days?"
4. "What's the cost of not solving this?"
5. "What kind of help are you looking for?"

### Rules
- Do not pitch during the Discovery Call
- Do not quote prices during the Discovery Call
- Do not commit to deliverables during the Discovery Call
- Listen. Take notes. Thank them.
- Follow up within 24 hours with a summary and next step

## PROPOSAL STANDARDS

All proposals are drafted by the Proposal Agent and approved by the Creator before sending.

### Required Proposal Elements
1. **Client's stated vision** — their words, not ours
2. **Recommended service** — specific, not vague
3. **Deliverables** — what they receive
4. **Timeline** — when they receive it
5. **Investment** — total price, payment structure
6. **Next step** — how to say yes

### Proposal Law
- No proposal sent without Creator approval
- No proposal outstanding more than 14 days without follow-up
- Every proposal has a defined expiration (standard: 21 days)
- A declined proposal is a learning event. Document the reason.
