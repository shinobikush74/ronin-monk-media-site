## THE LAW

Ronin Monk Media does not compete on price.  
Ronin Monk Media competes on transformation.

Every outreach answers one question:  
**"How can I make this person's business stronger?"**

No mass outreach. No spam. No discount selling.  
Acquire qualified clients through diagnosis, expertise, and trust.

## MODULE 1 — OPPORTUNITY DISCOVERY ENGINE

### Daily Research Target: 10 Qualified Businesses

**What to look for — businesses that have:**

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| Outdated website | Last redesigned 5+ years ago, mobile-broken, Flash remnants |
| Weak branding | Inconsistent colors, generic logos, no visual identity |
| Poor storytelling | "Welcome to our website." No personality, no mission |
| Weak calls-to-action | No clear next step for visitors |
| No collector ecosystem | No merchandise, digital products, or loyalty path |
| Poor mobile experience | Text overflow, broken navigation, tiny buttons |
| Weak search presence | No Google My Business, no local SEO |
| Inconsistent visual identity | 5 fonts, 8 colors, logo varies by page |

### Where to Find Qualified Opportunities

**United States — Primary**
- Google Maps: Search "[church / barbershop / tattoo studio] near [city]" — look at star ratings and website links
- Facebook Pages: Business pages with low engagement and weak visuals
- Instagram: Profiles with strong work but generic bios and no link-in-bio strategy
- Yelp / Google Business: Businesses with reviews but weak/missing websites
- LinkedIn: Small business owners with personal profiles but no company presence

**Community-Based Research**
- Local Facebook groups (business directories, neighborhood groups)
- Black Business directories (National Black Chamber of Commerce, local chapters)
- Church directories for denominations with multiple congregations
- Comic convention exhibitor lists
- Local arts council directories

| Type | Why They Qualify | Primary Pain | Best Service |
|------|-----------------|--------------|-------------|
| Churches | Strong community, weak digital | No online giving, can't attract younger members | Digital Presence + Story System |
| Independent Authors | Creative output, zero platform | No email list, Gumroad account, or brand voice | Publishing Ecosystem + Signal Calibration |

## MODULE 2 — FIVE MINUTE AUDIT ENGINE

See: [FIVE_MINUTE_AUDIT_TEMPLATE.md](FIVE_MINUTE_AUDIT_TEMPLATE.md)

### Audit Volume Target: 3 per day

**The audit rule:** Never criticize. Demonstrate opportunity.

Every audit must contain:
1. Current strengths (genuine — find them)
2. Three highest-impact improvements (specific)
3. Expected business benefit (concrete, not vague)
4. Invitation to schedule a consultation (single, clear)

**Delivery method:** Email or DM. Never dump the whole audit upfront. Lead with one key finding, offer the full audit as the reason to get on a call.

See: [TRANSFORMATION_PROPOSAL_FRAMEWORK.md](TRANSFORMATION_PROPOSAL_FRAMEWORK.md)

### Do not sell websites. Sell transformation.

**Service Categories for Proposals**

| Category | What It Includes | Entry Price |
|----------|-----------------|-------------|

| Digital Presence Design | Website — full story-driven build | Custom |

| Collector Ecosystem Design | Digital products, gated content, loyalty path | Custom |
| Creative Identity Systems | Full brand kit for creative professionals | $750+ |
| Worldbuilding Consultation | Universe architecture, lore structure | $125/session |
| Narrative Brand Strategy | Brand voice, positioning, messaging system | $275–$350 |
| Publishing Ecosystem Design | Gumroad setup, email list, content system | Custom |

Every proposal explains:
- **Current State** — what they have now (neutral, not dismissive)
- **Future State** — what becomes possible (specific, visual, compelling)
- **Business Value** — what this does for revenue, trust, community
- **Timeline** — realistic phases
- **Investment** — clear pricing, payment structure
- **Expected Outcomes** — 3–5 measurable results

## MODULE 4 — RELATIONSHIP ENGINE

### Every prospect receives exactly this sequence

| Contact | Timing | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Initial contact | Day 1 | Introduce, demonstrate value, invite |
| Follow-up One | Day 4 | Share one relevant resource or insight |
| Follow-up Two | Day 10 | Final reach — ask if timing is right |
| Educational resource | Within the sequence | A piece of authority content relevant to their situation |
| Final check-in | Day 21 | Close the file if no response — leave door open |

**No pressure. No desperation. Always provide value.**

Archive after Day 21. Revisit in 90 days with new content, not a sales message.

## MODULE 5 — AUTHORITY ENGINE

See: [AUTHORITY_CONTENT_SYSTEM.md](AUTHORITY_CONTENT_SYSTEM.md)

### Publish 1 authority piece per day

Every outreach campaign creates authority. The market discovers expertise before it sees pricing.

**Content Types**
- Website audits (public breakdowns of real examples, no client names)
- Brand breakdowns (what works and why)
- Before/After examples (with permission or using public cases)
- Educational articles (specific, opinionated, actionable)
- Short videos (screen recordings of audit findings)
- Client success stories (with permission, after delivery)
- Case studies (full transformation arc: before → process → after → outcome)

## EXAMPLE OUTREACH MESSAGES

### Outreach Example 1 — Barbershop

**Channel:** Instagram DM  
**Target:** Local barbershop with strong portfolio, no website

> [Name], I found [Shop Name] looking through barbershops in [City] and I spent some time on your page.
>
> The work is clearly there. What's not there is a way for someone who doesn't already know you to trust you before they sit in your chair.
>
> I do a quick 5-minute audit for small businesses — just a look at what's working and what one change would do most for your business. No charge.
>
> Worth a quick call if you're open to it.

### Outreach Example 3 — Church

**Channel:** Email to administrative contact  
**Target:** Church with no online giving, weak website, aging congregation

> Subject: One observation about [Church Name]'s digital presence

> [Pastor / Administrator Name],

> I visited [Church Name]'s website while researching faith communities in [City]. There's clearly a strong congregation — the history and mission of the church come through even in the existing site.

>
> I help churches and community organizations strengthen their digital presence so their mission reaches more people. I put together a brief audit with three specific improvements that would help [Church Name] serve its community more effectively online.
>
> Would a 15-minute conversation be worth your time?
>
> [Name]  
> Ronin Monk Media LLC
