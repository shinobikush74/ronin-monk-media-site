## PURPOSE

The Five Minute Audit is the primary trust-building tool in client acquisition. It demonstrates expertise before a prospect is asked to spend a dollar. It replaces cold pitching with diagnosis.

**The rule:** Never criticize. Demonstrate opportunity.

## COMPLETED EXAMPLE AUDIT 1 — Barbershop

**Business:** Classic Cuts Barbershop (example)  
**City:** Baltimore, MD  
**Research method:** Google Maps search, Instagram visit, Yelp check

---

**FIVE MINUTE AUDIT**  
Classic Cuts Barbershop  
Prepared by Ronin Monk Media LLC

**CURRENT STRENGTHS**

- 4.8 stars on Google with 94 reviews — the community trusts this shop
- Instagram portfolio shows exceptional fade and line work — the quality of the craft is clear
- Long operating history (est. 2009) — this is an institution, not a startup

**TOP THREE OPPORTUNITIES**

**Opportunity 1: No Website — Invisible to Anyone Who Doesn't Already Know You**  
What it is: Classic Cuts has no web presence beyond Google Business and Instagram. A new customer searching "best barbershop in Baltimore" will land on competitors with websites before they find you.  
What it costs them: Estimated 15–25% of potential new clients every month — people who saw one picture and couldn't find enough to make a decision.  
What becomes possible: A simple, story-driven website with a portfolio gallery, booking option, and shop story would convert browsers into first-time clients and first-timers into regulars.

**Opportunity 2: No Online Booking — Every Appointment Is an Extra Friction Point**  
What it is: Booking appears to be phone-only. No link in the Instagram bio to a booking system.  
What it costs them: Missed appointments from people who saw the feed at 11pm and couldn't book in the moment. A booked chair is a paid chair.  
What becomes possible: An integrated booking link (even a free Calendly or Square Appointments link) would capture late-night decisions that currently go cold.

**Opportunity 3: No Brand Story — The Shop Has History, but the World Doesn't Know It**  
What it is: The Instagram bio reads: "Classic Cuts Barbershop. Walk-ins welcome. DM to book." No year founded, no story, no personality.  
What it costs them: The shop is a neighborhood institution. That story is trust currency — and right now it's being left on the table.  
What becomes possible: A one-paragraph shop story (on the website, in the bio, on a Google Business update) would make every new visitor feel like they're joining something, not just getting a haircut.

**HIGHEST IMPACT NEXT STEP**  
Add a booking link to the Instagram bio and Google Business page. This is the fastest path to converting existing Instagram traffic into appointments.

**INVITATION**  
I put this together in five minutes — a full conversation would let me show you what a complete digital transformation looks like for a shop like yours. Worth 15 minutes if you're open to it.

## COMPLETED EXAMPLE AUDIT 2 — Independent Author

**Business:** Jordan Maxwell — Self-published fantasy author (example)  
**Platform:** Amazon KDP, personal blog (last updated 2023), Instagram (2,100 followers)  
**Research method:** Amazon author page, Instagram, Google search

---

**FIVE MINUTE AUDIT**  
Jordan Maxwell, Author  
Prepared by Ronin Monk Media LLC

**CURRENT STRENGTHS**

- Two published novels with verified Amazon reviews — a real reader base exists
- Instagram shows genuine engagement (comments on world lore, reader theories) — the audience is invested
- The writing itself is described in reviews as "cinematic" and "immersive" — strong product foundation

**TOP THREE OPPORTUNITIES**

**Opportunity 1: No Email List — The Reader Relationship Lives on Amazon's Platform, Not Yours**  
What it is: There is no visible way for a reader who finishes the book to stay connected to you. No newsletter signup, no community hub, no owned channel.  
What it costs them: Every reader who finishes the book is a warm prospect for the next book — but without an email list, there is no announcement channel when that book releases. Amazon does not share customer data with authors.  
What becomes possible: A simple landing page with a free chapter or exclusive lore document as a signup incentive would start converting readers into subscribers. 500 subscribers at 30% open rate means 150 people reading every announcement you send.

**Opportunity 2: No Digital Products — The Universe Has No Collectible Layer**  
What it is: The lore is rich enough to support additional products — maps, lore documents, character dossiers, short stories set in the same world. None of these exist.  
What it costs them: Readers who are invested in the world have nowhere to spend. The gap between a $12 paperback and the next $12 paperback is a dead zone.  
What becomes possible: A $5 PDF lore document or world map on Gumroad would create a collector pathway between books and give fans a way to deepen their investment in the universe.

**Opportunity 3: No Author Platform — The Brand Is "Just Another Amazon Page"**  
What it is: The personal blog exists but hasn't been updated in over a year. The Instagram has engagement but no direction (no links, no calls to action, no path forward for interested followers).  
What it costs them: A reader who loves the first book and goes looking for more will find a cold blog and an Instagram account that doesn't give them anywhere to go.  
What becomes possible: A simple author hub (clean website, active newsletter, Gumroad shop, linked Instagram) would create a platform the author owns — not one that lives inside Amazon's algorithm.

**HIGHEST IMPACT NEXT STEP**  
Set up a free Mailchimp or ConvertKit account and create a signup form with a free exclusive lore piece as the incentive. Post it on Instagram with a direct CTA. Start building the list while the audience exists.

**INVITATION**  
I build publishing ecosystems for independent authors — the platform, the reader community, the collector layer. Happy to show you what that looks like for your universe specifically. 15 minutes is all it takes.

## COMPLETED EXAMPLE AUDIT 3 — Church

**Business:** New Covenant Community Church (example)  
**City:** Atlanta, GA  
**Research method:** Google Maps, church website, Facebook Page

---

**FIVE MINUTE AUDIT**  
New Covenant Community Church  
Prepared by Ronin Monk Media LLC

**CURRENT STRENGTHS**

- 23 years in the community — a generational legacy of service
- Active Facebook Page with 1,400 followers and regular service updates
- Clear mission statement ("Restore, Rebuild, Revive") — strong brand foundation that just needs amplification

**TOP THREE OPPORTUNITIES**

**Opportunity 1: No Online Giving — Digital Generosity Has No Path**  
What it is: The website has no digital giving option. No donate button, no Tithe.ly link, no Venmo or Cash App QR code on any page.  
What it costs them: An estimated 30–40% of churchgoers now prefer to give digitally. Younger members especially will not write a check. Without a digital giving option, the church is leaving tithes in pockets.  
What becomes possible: A simple digital giving integration (Tithe.ly is free to set up and charges the donor a processing fee) would open the giving channel to members who want to give but have no mechanism. This is typically the highest ROI improvement a church can make.

**Opportunity 2: The Website Does Not Welcome a First-Time Visitor**  
What it is: The current website opens with a sermon archive from eight months ago. There is no "I'm new here" section, no service time that's easy to find, no photo of the congregation or pastor.  
What it costs them: Someone in crisis — which is often why people search for a new church — will not find a reason to visit. The first impression fails to represent the warmth that the congregation clearly has based on their Facebook comments.  
What becomes possible: A single "New Here?" page with service times, a welcome video from the pastor, and a map would convert curious visitors into first-time attendees. This is the church's most important conversion page.

**Opportunity 3: No Mobile-Optimized Experience**  
What it is: On a mobile device, the homepage navigation breaks and the text overlaps the header image. More than 70% of church website visits happen on mobile.  
What it costs them: Every visitor on a phone is seeing a broken version of the church before they've set foot in the building.  
What becomes possible: A mobile-first redesign would ensure the church's digital presence matches the quality of the in-person experience.

**HIGHEST IMPACT NEXT STEP**  
Set up a Tithe.ly or Venmo/Cash App link and place it prominently on the website homepage and Facebook Page. This can be done in one afternoon and has immediate impact on digital giving.

**INVITATION**  
I help churches strengthen their digital presence so their mission reaches more people. I'd be glad to show you what a full transformation would look like for New Covenant. A 15-minute call is all it takes to get started.
