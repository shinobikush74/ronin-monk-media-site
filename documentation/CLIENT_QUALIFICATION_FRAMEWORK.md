## PURPOSE

Every minute spent pursuing an unqualified prospect is a minute not spent serving a client who is ready.

This framework ensures that outreach energy goes where it can convert — and that proposals go only to prospects who are positioned to say yes.

---

## QUALIFICATION TIERS

### Tier A — Ready to Engage (Pursue Immediately)

**Signals:**
- Has an existing website that is clearly outdated or broken
- Has active social media but zero platform infrastructure (no email list, no landing page)
- Has revenue or community (they are operational) but the digital presence doesn't reflect it
- Has expressed frustration about their digital situation (posts, comments, reviews)
- Has a mission or creative body of work that deserves better packaging

**Action:** Send personalized outreach within 24 hours of identification. Prepare audit first.

---

### Tier B — Promising but Needs Development (Nurture)

**Signals:**
- Business exists but hard to determine if digital investment is a priority
- Decision-maker is identified but hasn't signaled readiness
- Business is early-stage (less than 1 year) and may not have budget yet
- Positive social engagement but no clear revenue base

**Action:** Add to nurture sequence. Send educational content. Revisit in 30 days.

---

### Tier C — Not Ready (Park for 90 Days)

**Signals:**
- No website, no social media, no search presence at all (not yet thinking digitally)
- Franchise or chain location with locked branding (can't make decisions)
- Appears to be going out of business or inactive
- Has a brand-new website or just finished a redesign

**Action:** Log in CRM. Set 90-day reminder. Do not pursue now.

---

### Disqualified — Do Not Pursue

**Signals:**
- Competes entirely on lowest price (race to the bottom)
- Has publicly dismissed the value of professional branding / websites
- Prior inquiry that ended with hostility or wasted time
- Business model that conflicts with the values in the Constitution

**Action:** Log and move on. Do not re-engage.

## THE FIVE QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS

Before sending outreach, answer these five questions. If three or more answers are "no," move to the next prospect.

1. **Does this business have something worth transforming?**  
   Is there a real mission, real work, real community? Or is it a shell?

2. **Is the gap visible?**  
   Can I see the gap between what they have and what they could have? Can I name it specifically?

3. **Is the decision-maker reachable?**  
   Can I find an email, DM, or direct contact path — not a generic info@ that goes nowhere?

4. **Does this prospect fit a service we already deliver?**  
   Is there a clear service match from the catalog, or would this require building something entirely new?

5. **Would I be proud to show this work as a case study?**  
   Is this a client and project I would want associated with Ronin Monk Media?

## BUDGET REALITY CHECK

Never lead with price. But understand the reality before investing significant proposal time.

**Churches:** Budgets vary widely. Large congregations have facilities and technology budgets. Small churches may be tight — but they often have community fundraising capacity. Approach: start with a scoped entry project.

**Independent Authors / Musicians / Comic Creators:** Often bootstrapped. But if they have a back catalog and a community, they have a path. Approach: offer phased payment or artifact-first entry.

**Barbershops / Tattoo Studios:** Operators, not investors. Strong cash flow but skeptical of intangibles. Approach: frame around revenue impact, not aesthetics.

**Cultural Organizations / Museums:** Grant-funded. Budget cycles. Approach: understand their grant calendar. A proposal timed to budget season converts.

**Black-Owned Small Businesses:** Under-resourced compared to competitors but highly motivated. Approach: emphasize the competitive advantage of a stronger digital presence.

## DISQUALIFICATION PROTOCOL

If a prospect shows any of the following during outreach or a Discovery Call:

- "I just need something cheap and fast"
- "My nephew can do it for free"
- "I don't believe in paying for a website"
- Haggling on the first conversation before scope is even defined
- Aggressive, dismissive, or disrespectful communication

**Immediately disengage. Politely.**

> "It sounds like what I offer may not be the right fit right now. If that changes, the door is open."

Log the interaction. Do not follow up.

## IDEAL CLIENT PROFILE

The ideal Ronin Monk Media client:

- Believes in the quality of their work and wants the world to see it that way
- Is frustrated that their digital presence doesn't match their real-world reputation
- Is willing to invest in the right infrastructure, not the cheapest option
- Has a community, audience, or congregation that would grow with a stronger platform
- Values the process, not just the deliverable
- Would refer other clients if the work is strong

When a prospect matches this profile, move fast. These are the ones.
