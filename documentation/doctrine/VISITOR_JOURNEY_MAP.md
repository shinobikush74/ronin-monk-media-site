# VISITOR JOURNEY MAP
## How People Move Through the Ronin Monk Headquarters
**Established:** 2026-06-26
**Status:** Active — informs all navigation design and content placement

---

## I. THE CORE TRUTH

Visitors do not use a website the way users use software.

They arrive with a state of mind. They carry that state through the experience. They leave changed — or they leave unchanged. The Headquarters is designed to make the first outcome more likely than the second.

There is no funnel. There is a path.

A funnel pushes visitors toward a transaction.
A path invites travelers toward a destination.

The distinction changes every design decision.

---

## II. THE FIVE ARCHETYPES

### 1. The Seeker
**Who they are:** Someone in a state of searching. They are looking for something they cannot yet name — a philosophy, a community, a vocabulary for something they already know.

**Where they enter:** The Threshold (homepage) — most likely from social media or a direct referral.

**What they need:** An experience that gives them language for what they already feel. They need to feel recognized before they feel invited.

**Journey:**
The Threshold → The Doctrine (Zone 1) → One frequency that matches their pull → Discovery in that frequency → The Order (join)

**What makes them stay:** The mantra. When they read "Every story is a weapon against hopelessness" and feel it is true, they know they are in the right place.

**What makes them leave:** Generic language. Feeling like they've landed on another creative agency page.

---

### 2. The Collaborator
**Who they are:** A creative professional — writer, designer, musician, brand builder — who needs what Ronin Monk Media offers and is deciding whether to trust it.

**Where they enter:** The Forge (Services) or The Threshold — most likely from direct outreach or LinkedIn/professional referral.

**What they need:** To see evidence of world. Not just competence — vision. They need to believe that Ronin Monk Media sees something they want to be part of.

**Journey:**
The Threshold → The Forge → Service descriptions as transformation → The Council Chamber (contact)

**What makes them convert:** The artifact framing and transformation language. "World Awakening" communicates something different from "Worldbuilding Consultation." They feel the difference.

**What makes them leave:** Generic portfolio with deliverables listed. That is everywhere. This must be nowhere else.

---

### 3. The Listener
**Who they are:** A music fan — specifically someone who responds to the Shinobi Kush frequency. Hip-hop adjacent, spiritually curious, culturally grounded.

**Where they enter:** The Resonance Chamber (Music) — most likely from Bandcamp, SoundCloud, or social media music post.

**What they need:** To hear, then to understand. The music brought them. The Headquarters gives them the world the music comes from.

**Journey:**
The Resonance Chamber → Ambient audio plays → Lore fragment draws them → The Living Mythology → The Archive (some will read) → The Order (join)

**What makes them stay:** The connection between the music and a larger universe. Most artists have music on a music page. This artist has a world. The Listener wants to live in that world.

**What makes them leave:** The music page is treated as a streaming aggregator. No atmosphere. No mythology. Just links.

---

### 4. The Lore Reader
**Who they are:** A speculative fiction fan, worldbuilder, game designer, or mythology enthusiast. They are drawn to the Sky Khan universe itself.

**Where they enter:** The Living Mythology or The Archive — most likely from a Google search, a Reddit post, or a fan referral.

**What they need:** Depth. More. They are not reading to consume — they are reading to inhabit. They will follow every link. They will find every hidden fragment. They will return.

**Journey:**
The Living Mythology → Faction lore → Character lore → Hidden discovery elements → The Archive (essays and field notes) → Codex (purchased) → The Order (joined)

**What makes them stay:** The universe feels real. It has internal logic. It has history. There are things they don't yet know that feel knowable.

**What makes them leave:** The lore is thin. One page of "coming soon." The universe stops before they do.

---

### 5. The Carrier
**Who they are:** Someone who has already encountered the work — been to the Headquarters before, heard the music, read a blog post. They return. They share. They recruit others.

**Where they enter:** Directly — typed URL, bookmark, or newsletter link.

**What they need:** New transmission. Something that was not here before. A reason to return and a reason to send others.

**Journey:**
The Threshold (familiar now) → What's new in The Archive or The Resonance Chamber → Discovery of new hidden element → The Fifth Frequency (ACTION) → Share

**What makes them return:** The Headquarters is alive. Something changes. New transmissions. New lore fragments. New discoveries. The world grows.

**What makes them stop returning:** Stasis. The same site, unchanged, every time.

## III. THE EMOTIONAL ARC

Every visitor passes through these states, in some order:

| State | What triggers it | What it feels like |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Arrival** | First view of The Threshold | "I am somewhere. I don't yet know what it is." |
| **Recognition** | Reading the mantra or doctrine | "Someone here knows what I have felt." |
| **Orientation** | The Four Frequencies or nav | "I understand where to go." |
| **Immersion** | Entering a frequency page | "I am inside something." |
| **Discovery** | Finding a hidden element | "This place rewards me for looking." |
| **Belonging** | The Order zone | "There are others here." |
| **Invitation** | Any CTA in doctrine language | "There is somewhere I can go from here." |

The Headquarters must support all seven states for every archetype.

Missing states by archetype:
- **Seeker** — Recognition is critical. If they do not feel recognized in the first 60 seconds, they leave.
- **Collaborator** — Orientation is critical. They need to find The Forge within 2 clicks of entry.
- **Listener** — Immersion is critical. Audio must be available without friction.
- **Lore Reader** — Discovery is critical. They must find at least one thing that was not obvious.
- **Carrier** — Invitation is critical. They need to know what to share and how.

## IV. THE PATH (NOT THE FUNNEL)

The path is not linear. It is a world with multiple entry points.

```
The Threshold
     |
     ├── WORD → The Archive
     ├── SOUND → The Resonance Chamber
     ├── SYMBOL → The Forge
     ├── STORY → The Living Mythology
     └── ACTION → The Fifth Frequency
                        |
                        └── The Order (from any page via footer + Zone 5)
                                   |
                                   └── The Marketplace of Relics
                                   └── The Council Chamber
```

The Transmission Thread at the bottom of every page provides three curated exits — always a next step, never a dead end. This is the mechanism that keeps travelers moving through the world rather than bouncing out.

---

## V. THE RETURN DOCTRINE

The Headquarters is not designed for a single visit. It is designed for **return**.

Mechanisms that create return:
1. **New transmissions** — Blog posts, new music, new lore fragments
2. **Hidden elements** — Not everything is visible on first visit; visitors may return to find what they missed
3. **The Order** — Email transmissions bring Carriers back
4. **The Fifth Frequency** — Community events, missions, and workshops create calendared return
5. **The Marketplace** — New artifacts released in limited quantities create scarcity-based return
